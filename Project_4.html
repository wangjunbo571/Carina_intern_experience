SMS Service

The Week 1 design is focused on allowing people to customize their time schedules that they need health care. Then health care provider can apply the jobs that match their own schedules

This step of setting the availability is in the process of posting a job.

Firstly, people will need to enter "How many hours are you allotted monthly"

Then there is a schedule table. People can click and drag on the table to change the color of elements. It will illustrate the time schedule of the health care seeker

Finally, there is a section to show how many hours have been selected and how many more hours you need to select in order to match your alloted hours

The Week 2 design is focused on allowing people to match their customized time schedule with the jobs' schedule.

This is in the process that healthcare providers want to apply for a job. In the Jobboard, there will be plenty of Jobcards

In each job card, it will show the matching hours of this job with your schedule.

For each day, one corresponding blue circle will show the matching hours. If it is no matching hours, it will be grey

When users hover over the schedule circles, it will show the details of matching hours in the form of schedule table

The Week 3 design is focused on the matching features

Firstly, a more comprehensive profile settings is designed

The diversity of health care provider and seekers will be considered.

They can find the most matching services through the increased diversity settings

From the form on the left, job applicants can chose what kind of jobs they want

Then jobcards will show whether this jobcard match that criteria by cross or tick

The unimportant area, which are not chosen, will be hidden.

When people do not chose any filter criteria, jobcards will show all the information

Users can more easily find the matching health care providers in Carina

The Week 4 design is focused on the SMS service, through it users can update their availability.

The Carina will send out prompt messages to users to check their availability, based on their attributes

Then users will return their message to express their availability status

The Carina server will update the availability status correspondingly

The Week 5 design is still focused on the SMS service

In the past, we developed the applications independently from the Carina Care application

In this week, we touch the real Carina application and get familiar with the Java Spring

And the SMS service is designed more comprehensively

The Week 6 is more daily job rather than the previous weekly jobs to update the websites of Carina

Firstly, we touched the database and added an column to store the last updated time

After that, we set up the preview of the photo that clients want to upload as a profile photos

And the SMS service is designed more comprehensively

The Week 7 is still focusing on UX stories to update the websites of Carina
